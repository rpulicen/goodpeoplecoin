Sponsored Listings on GoodPeopleCoin

This page explains how sponsored listings work on GoodPeopleCoin. We only consider projects with a charity or impact focus and clear donation structures. All sponsored placements are clearly labeled and kept separate from general educational content.

Independent & selective

What a sponsored listing includes

Sponsored listings are research-style placements for teams that want additional visibility in front of people who care about verifiable impact.

  • Project overview written in a neutral, descriptive tone
  • Mention of your donation wallet structure and transparency practices
  • Placement in the "Projects we track" area on the homepage (sponsored label)
  • Link to your official site and / or docs
  • Optional: one short mention on the @goodpeoplecoin account

A sponsored listing does not mean we are endorsing or recommending your token. It simply means we have reviewed the basic information you provided and are disclosing that your placement is paid.

Minimum requirements

What we look for before considering a listing

  • Clear charity or impact angle (who benefits, how often, how funds move)
  • Donation wallets that can be shared publicly
  • Ability to provide receipts, TX hashes, or other proof of impact over time
  • No obvious honeypot mechanics or opaque tokenomics
  • Agreement that coverage will remain neutral and clearly labeled as sponsored

We reserve the right to decline any project that doesn't fit the focus or risk profile of this site.

Frequently asked questions

Do you guarantee any results or token performance?
No. A sponsored listing is a visibility product, not a guarantee of performance. We do not comment on price, upside, or future performance.
Can you change something if our project structure evolves?
We can adjust factual details over time (such as donation wallet addresses or updated impact reports) as part of an ongoing listing, as long as the core agreement remains intact.
How do we start the process?
Email ads@goodpeoplecoin.com with your project name, links, donation structure, and which tier you are interested in. We'll reply with a short questionnaire and current availability.
Can you refuse or remove a listing?
Yes. We reserve the right to decline or remove listings that no longer fit the focus or risk tolerance of this site, especially if we see misleading claims or major undisclosed changes.
