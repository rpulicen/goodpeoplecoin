How GoodPeopleCoin Works

GoodPeopleCoin is a small, independent project focused on charity and impact-focused crypto. This page explains what we cover, how we think about transparency, and how sponsored listings are handled.

Scope of coverage

What we actually look at

The main focus of GoodPeopleCoin is projects that claim some mix of:

  • Charity or nonprofit donations
  • Impact or public goods funding (on- or off-chain)
  • Donation wallets, treasury wallets, or on-chain proofs of impact

We do not try to track every meme coin or speculative token. Instead, we pay attention to projects that at least attempt to connect token activity with real-world outcomes or public goods.

Independence & sponsored content

How "sponsored listings" work

GoodPeopleCoin is not an exchange, a launchpad, or a fundraising platform. We do not custody funds. For a limited number of teams, we offer clearly labeled sponsored placements:

  • Sponsored cards or write-ups are always labeled as sponsored.
  • Coverage is descriptive, not promotional or predictive.
  • We do not comment on price targets or upside.
  • We reserve the right to decline or remove listings at our discretion.

Sponsored coverage does not mean we have audited or endorsed a project. It simply means we have reviewed baseline information and agreed to describe it in a neutral tone with disclosures.

How we review basic project information

For both general write-ups and sponsored placements, we typically look at a few simple things:

  • Whether there is a clear description of the charity or impact goal.
  • Whether donation wallets are shared and, where possible, visible on-chain.
  • Whether there are any public receipts, reports, or proofs of past impact.
  • Whether tokenomics or mechanics are explained in a way a normal reader can understand.

We do not perform smart contract audits, regulatory checks, or deep technical due diligence. Readers should treat all pages on this site as starting points for their own research, not as final answers.

What we don't do

  • We do not provide financial, investment, tax, or legal advice.
  • We do not offer personalized recommendations on which tokens to buy or sell.
  • We do not guarantee the behavior, honesty, or long-term viability of any project.
  • We do not run fundraising campaigns or accept user deposits on behalf of other projects.

Crypto assets are volatile and risky. Charity- or impact-themed tokens can fail, pivot, or stop donating altogether. Anyone considering getting involved with a project should consult professionals and double-check information directly with the relevant organizations.

Transparency and corrections

If a project we mention updates its structure, changes its donation wallets, or releases new impact reports, we may update existing pages to reflect those facts over time. We also welcome corrections and clarifications from teams and readers.

If you see something that looks inaccurate, you can reach out via: ads@goodpeoplecoin.com or on X at @goodpeoplecoin.
